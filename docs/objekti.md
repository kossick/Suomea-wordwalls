```mermaid
---
title: Choosing the correct objective noun or pronoun ending
---
flowchart TD
  A(Start)
  B{Is the verb\nnegative?}
  C[Use the partitive]
  D{Does the verb\nrequire a case?}
  E{Partitive or\naccusative?}
  F{Is it a\npronoun?}
  G([Depends on length/\ncompleteness of\ndescribed process])
  H[Use the accusative]
  I[Use '-t' ending]
  J{Singular\nor plural?}
  K{Is it an\nimperative\nsentence?}
  L[Use '-n' ending]
  M["Use nominative\n(dictionary) form"]

  A --- B
  B -- Yes (1) --> C 
  B -- No --> D
  D -- Yes --> E
  E -- Partitive\n(ex: odottaa) (2) --> C
  E -- Accusative\n(ex: tavata) --> F
  D -- Depends on\ncontext, or no\n(ex: ostaa) --> G
  G -- Short/completed (3) --> H
  G -- Lengthy/repeated/\nongoing/several (4) --> C
  F -- Yes (ex: hän/he) (5) --> I
  F -- No --> J
  J -- Plural (6) --> I
  J -- Singular  --> K
  K -- No (7) --> L
  K -- Yes (ex: Juo!) (8*) --> M
```

## Example sentences

(1) Minulla ei ole autoa/Älä juo olutta!

(2) Minä odotan häntä/Lisaa

(3) Ostan omenan (an apple)/omenat (apples)

(4) Ostan omenaa (some apple)/omenoita (some apples)

(5) Minä tapaan hänet/heidät

(6) Minä tapaan vanhemmat

(7) Minä tapaan Lisan

(8) Juo kahvi!

*: this could still be paritive if it is _some_ of the noun e.g. Juo kahvia!
